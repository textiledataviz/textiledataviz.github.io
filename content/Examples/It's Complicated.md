Natasha Hollenbach did a [[Surface Embroidery]] piece, "[It's Complicated](https://openbooks.library.umass.edu/unseen-labor-exhibit/chapter/natasha-hollenbach/)" showing some of the ways that metadata is complicated. Each letter represents group of things (the [[organization]] property):

-   M: The Five Types of Metadata (with the words Administrative, Descriptive, Technical, Structural, Preservation stitched inside)
-   E: Searching (Boolean, Facets, Sorting, Keywords, And Or, Filters, Exact Phrase)
-   T: Metadata Standards ([BIBFRAME](https://www.loc.gov/bibframe/), [Dublin Core](https://dublincore.org/), [MARC](https://www.loc.gov/marc/), [RDA](https://www.loc.gov/aba/rda/), [METS](http://www.loc.gov/standards/mets/), [EAD](https://www.loc.gov/ead/), MODS)
-   A: User Interactions (Usable, Interface, Find, Online, Discoverable, Accessible)
-   D: Current Trends (Crowdsourcing, [Linked Data](https://www.w3.org/standards/semanticweb/data), Tags, CAD, [OCR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_character_recognition), [HTR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Handwriting_recognition), [Digital Humanities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_humanities), [API)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/API)
-   A: Maintenance (Legacy Data, Updates, Clean-up, Migration, Fix, New Term)
-   T: Controlled Vocabularies ([TGM](https://www.loc.gov/rr/print/tgm1/), [TGN](https://www.getty.edu/research/tools/vocabularies/tgn/), [LCNAF](https://id.loc.gov/authorities/names.html), [MeSH](https://www.nlm.nih.gov/mesh/meshhome.html), [LCSH](https://www.loc.gov/aba/cataloging/subject/), [NALT](https://agclass.nal.usda.gov/), CONA, [AAT)](https://www.getty.edu/research/tools/vocabularies/aat/)
-   A: Flexible/Consistent and Aggregate/Local (Flexible, Consistent, Focus, Details, Aggregate, Local) are the two big balances acts that we all perform with our metadata."
