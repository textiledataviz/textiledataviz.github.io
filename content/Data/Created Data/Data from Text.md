Text is a rich source material to adapt into data that you can visualize with textiles. One of the easiest approaches to look at occurrences or frequencies of different terms -- or alternately, the presence or absence of terms across different texts.

To do this, you need a digital, searchable version of the text(s) you're analyzing. Plain text (.txt) files are ideal. If you're not comfortable writing code, you can use a tool like [Voyant](https://voyant-tools.org/) to explore the texts, search for terms and phrases and identify their frequency across the text(s). (See this [Programming Historian tutorial on Voyant](https://programminghistorian.org/en/lessons/corpus-analysis-voyant-tools) for tips on how to get started.)

Examples of woven data from text include [Computers, Archives and Librarians in Star Trek](https://quinndaedal.com/textiles/computers-archives-librarians-star-trek/) and [The Locked Loom 1: Gideon the Ninth](https://quinndaedal.com/blog/2023/12/21/locked-loom-1-gideon-the-ninth/).
